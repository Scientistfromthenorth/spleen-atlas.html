White pulp follicles — lymphoid nodules rich in T and B cells — sit within the red pulp, separated by the marginal zone, the interface where blood-borne antigen is filtered.

Each region was independently microdissected and processed for MHC class I immunopeptidome and whole-proteome analysis, so peptide and protein content can be compared region to region rather than averaged across the whole organ.
